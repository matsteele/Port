Urban area population figures come from the East Asia Urbanization report by the World Bank. The urban area population counts were determined based on the visible urban extents of the urban areas and not based on official country-wide administrative boundaries. This means that some cities may appear larger than official figures. Unfortunately, the dataset only included cities in East Asia. To make this map more complete I added in cities in India, but the population figures reflect their official counts, and were not calculated based on urban extents.

Semerang School Catchments

The intention of the application was to present to the Semarang government ways they can make information accessible to their population.
Attempting to work with data in the context of a developing country means working with often poorly written legislative frameworks and extremely unreliable and incomplete databases. Many schools were missing and those that were reported were mis-labeled and mis-located. The unintended effect of the visualization of the school catchment areas was a demonstration of how contradictory the related legislative requirements are. A major starting point for every city we worked with was to execute a mayoral decree that, among other things, insisted that all city units use a consistent conception of administrative boundaries. The lower the administration the more politically contentious their definitions, resulting in multiple interpretations and data confusion. Considering this, the app was built to both demonstrate the legislation and data as it is, as well as provide a means for its constant improvement. By clicking on the edits tab, a user can contribute new data that can be integrated to the existing dataset. The edit button allows for the user to suggest changes to the schools data in order to create a more complete dataset.

Educational Achievement In Semarang

This study makes use of available demographic data in Indonesia to identify indicators for educational achievement in the city of Semarang. The study involved taking linear and regressions to identify statistically significant indicators among the demographic data available. The study also made use of data built from available spatial features, such as building footprints and raster imagery.

The study found interesting and surprisingly strong relationships between being a migrant and educational achievement, which contradicts the negative stereotypes we heard recounted by government officials regarding the issues immigrants pose to the city. It became a useful talking point in discussing slum management and the inflow of internal indonesian migrants.

Sao Paulo Social Housing Redevelopment Plan

I participated in an interdisciplinary studio focused on social housing in Sao Paulo, Brazil. My role was producing the economic and financial analysis for a redevelopment plan for Nova Luz, a central neighborhood in Sao Paulo, in partnership with AECOM. We were tasked with developing a strategy for housing development in the area that would be palatable to local community members after two previous versions of a master plan for the area were dismissed by community groups. The focus of the plan was to integrate social housing as a crux of all of the development proposals in the area, making use of existing public private financing strategies. The major intention of the plan is to change what is the largest open air drug market in Brazil. Our proposals suggested ways of framing the area as a center for public health and integrating lucrative government contracts for desperately needed health centers for the area.
